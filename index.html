Summary

I'm an interdisciplinary researcher and activist, researching the intersection of data protection/privacy (broadly), computer science, requirement engineering, security (broadly), and regulatory bodies. I got my PhD at the Université Paris 1 Panthéon-Sorbonne in requirements engineering, title "STAP - Socio-Technical dAta Protection framework for requirements with divergent mental models". I'll soon by starting as a research associate in cybersecurity at Interdisciplinary Research Group in Socio-technical Cybersecurity (https://irisc-lab.uni.lu/) - SnT of the University of Luxembourg. I also hold a Ms.C (distinction and honors) in Interactive Digital Media from Trinity College Dublin and a professional degree (5 years diploma) in Political Science from the Pontifical Catholic University of Chile.

My main area of interest is new technologies' requirements for data protection, cybersecurity and socio-technical challenges. My research in particular, takes an interdisciplinary and user-centered point of view on requirements, taking special consideration of regulation.

More about me

I'm also interested in OSINT, intelligence and security studies, however it isn't my current area of research. Recently I have started researching about responsible information systems. In the past I have TA'ed an introduction to programming (Python),and assisted in the requirement engineering, blockchain technology and research methodology classes at Paris 1. I'm also giving blockchain and data protection classes in other universities (both in Chile and France).

As part of my PhD program, I'm still participating in the Scholars Programme from the Europaeumm, which is two-years intensive trainning programme on policy and leadership.

I'm an active member of civil society, participating in different NGOs, particularly OptIA. OptIA is a South American NGO focused on algorithmic accountability, transparency, and inclusion. I've collaborated with various institutional bodies of different countries on topics relating to technology, presented in academic conferences, and given general public talks about technology/privacy in: radio shows, webinars, non-academic conferences, presentations to governmental bodies, and others. I was selected (somehow) as 100 young leader of Chile in 2022 (100 jóvenes líderes) You can find if I'll be participating in future events down below [on summer leave, I don't forsee many public engagement in the near future]. Sometimes I give online courses about blockchain or other tech for a very low price in Spanish (you can check if there is an upcoming class at this link or https://www.buymeacoffee.com/csnegri )

I'm an amateur photographer. In my spare time I like to drink coffee, listen to jazz, doing sports (bouldering if I can) and, travel. I also really enjoy City Pop and French touch... Currently based in Europe, however I'm a nomad.
